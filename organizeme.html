Problem

Daily disorganization leads to missed deadlines and inefficiency for many, overwhelmed by tasks, projects, and appointments.

Students often find it challenging to balance coursework, extracurricular activities, and personal commitments efficiently.

As a student pursuing Human-Computer Interaction in Munich while also managing a part-time role as a UX designer, I encounter the perpetual challenge of harmonizing academic demands, work commitments, and personal pursuits. Amidst project deadlines and coursework, finding time for social engagements, sports, and other extracurricular activities proves to be a constant juggling act. Despite leveraging organizational tools, maintaining equilibrium between academic rigor, professional growth, and a fulfilling personal life remains an ongoing endeavor.

Solution

From Chaos to Clarity: Organize ME Keeps Your Life in Sync!

Enter Organize ME, your ultimate productivity companion. Seamlessly manage tasks, projects, and schedules while effortlessly balancing academic commitments, work responsibilities, and personal interests. With intuitive features tailored for students like you, Organize ME streamlines organization, ensuring you stay on top of your game. Say goodbye to chaos and hello to productivity with Organize ME by your side.

Research

Part 1

User Research

To understand if fellow students face challenges with productivity and organization, I created a set of questions. These questions were designed to explore how they handle stress and plan their tasks. After narrowing down the list to 10 key questions, I conducted brief 15-minute interviews with 10 students. To gain insights from a broader perspective, I extended these questions to non-Students friends and family, aiming to capture diverse viewpoints beyond the student community.

  1. How do you prioritize your tasks and assignments?
  2. What methods or tools do you currently use to stay organized?
  3. How do you handle stress related to academic and personal responsibilities?
  4. Are there specific times of the day when you feel most productive?
  5. Do you struggle with procrastination, and if so, how do you overcome it?
  6. How do you balance academic commitments with work and social activities?
  7. What challenges do you face when planning and managing your schedule?
  8. Are there any specific goals or targets you set for yourself regularly?
  9. Are there any specific tools or features you wish you had to better organize your tasks?
  10. What functionalities or improvements do you believe would assist you in managing your academic and personal responsibilities more efficiently?

Part 2

Affinity Diagram

After conducting user research, I applied an affinity diagram approach to distill the collected data. Organizing statements, data points, and quotes on sticky notes revealed emergent patterns, allowing me to categorize them into meaningful groups. Each group was then assigned a relevant title, and from these clusters, I identified distinct problems and opportunities. The next step involved posing targeted questions to these problem and opportunity areas, leading to insightful discoveries that form the foundation for informed design decisions.

Part 3

How-Might-We

After analyzing the insights derived from the affinity diagram, my next step in the design process involved crafting 'How Might We' (HMW) statements. These statements served as a bridge from identified challenges and opportunities to creative solution exploration. Each HMW statement framed a specific aspect of the insights as an open-ended question, sparking my solo brainstorming sessions to generate a diverse range of potential solutions.

  • How might we develop productivity tools that allow for clear distinctions between different types of tasks, catering to users who value separation in their workflows?
  • How might we support students in implementing effective productivity strategies, such as breaking tasks into smaller steps, as a routine habit?
  • How might we improve digital tools to seamlessly integrate task lists and calendars, offering students a more unified organizational experience?
  • How might we enhance students' ability to prioritize tasks effectively without solely relying on deadlines?
  • How might we provide students with a more intuitive system for organizing assignments based on importance and personal goals?

Part 4

Findings

After analyzing the insights derived from the affinity diagram, my next step in the design process involved crafting 'How Might We' (HMW) statements. These statements served as a bridge from identified challenges and opportunities to creative solution exploration. Each HMW statement framed a specific aspect of the insights as an open-ended question, sparking my solo brainstorming sessions to generate a diverse range of potential solutions. Upon synthesizing these solutions, I used them to develop a persona, embodying the characteristics, needs, and behaviors identified through the analysis, thus providing a tangible representation of the user base and guiding further design decisions.

Ideation/Design

Part 1

Ideation

The ideation process for "Organize Me" commenced with the implementation of the "Crazy 8" method, followed by conventional brainstorming sessions. This initial phase aimed to generate diverse concepts efficiently. To further enrich the ideation process, external sources such as Pinterest, magazines, and existing applications were explored for inspiration regarding color schemes, typography, and overall design aesthetics. These platforms facilitated the gathering of visual stimuli, aiding in the formulation of potential directions for the app's design. Specific focus was placed on analyzing color combinations, font styles, and overall ambiance to create a cohesive and visually appealing user interface. This phase primarily emphasized the extraction of design elements rather than subjective expressions of creativity. Through methodical selection and refinement, the ideation phase culminated in the establishment of a distinct vibe for "Organize Me," aligning with its intended purpose and target audience. In summary, the ideation process for "Organize Me" prioritized systematic exploration and extraction of design elements from various sources, resulting in the development of a coherent and visually engaging concept for the app's portfolio.

Part 2

User Flow Diagram

The user flow diagram for TaskMentor visually illustrates the sequential steps a user takes within the app, from onboarding to achieving specific goals like prioritizing tasks and utilizing productivity tools. This diagram serves as a comprehensive roadmap, providing a clear and concise overview of the app's functionalities, user interactions, and the logical progression through various features, facilitating both user comprehension and strategic development decisions

Part 3

Wireframing

My next step was wireframing — a step where I'm sketching out the basic structure before diving into detailed design. This helps me focus on the essential features, making sure the app is easy to use. Once I've got the wireframes, I can quickly test them with users to catch any issues early on, making sure the app is user-friendly and accessible as I move towards the final version."

Part 4

Changes

After creating wireframes for Organize ME, I decided to gather feedback from friends and family by having them test it out. Their input was invaluable, as it highlighted areas that needed improvement. For instance, some found certain features confusing or difficult to navigate. As a result, I went back to the drawing board, refining the wireframes based on their feedback. This iterative process led to a more user-friendly design that better aligned with the needs and expectations of potential users.

Part 4

First Prototypes

While adhering to the wireframing phase, I noticed elements that required adjustment or didn't aesthetically align with my vision. This involved several rounds of refining designs, color combinations, and layouts. On the left side, I gathered earlier components and color schemes for comparison. Each adjustment aimed for a balance between aesthetics and functionality. This iterative process allowed for the development of a cohesive prototype, ensuring each element served its purpose effectively.

Conclusion

Reflecting on the Journey: The Creation of Organize ME.

The development of Organize ME was an enlightening journey that underscored the importance of iterative design and user feedback. Starting from initial brainstorming sessions to creating detailed personas, every step was driven by a commitment to understanding and addressing user needs. Testing with friends and family provided crucial insights that refined the app's functionality and usability. Although the documentation wasn't planned to be as thorough, and some parts may be missing or not perfectly described, the project successfully resulted in a user-friendly, efficient tool for managing tasks, projects, and schedules. This project not only honed my skills in UX design but also reinforced the value of empathy and user-centered design in creating meaningful digital solutions.
